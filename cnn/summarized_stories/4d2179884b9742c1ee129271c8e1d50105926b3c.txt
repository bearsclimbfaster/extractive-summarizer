Burkina Faso's military on Saturday backed one of its officers to lead the country temporarily, seemingly settling what had been two officers' claims to authority after the West African nation's longtime president resigned. The military's leadership will support Lt. Col. Isaac Yacouba Zida as interim President until elections can be held, according to a military statement broadcast by the country's TV stations.
