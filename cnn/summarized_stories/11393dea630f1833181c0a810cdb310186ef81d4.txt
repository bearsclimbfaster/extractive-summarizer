For some athletes, running the Marine Corps Marathon is about the exhilaration of running 26.2 miles, pushing through one's physical limits and the thrill of taking those first steps past the finish line. But for other runners, like Salina Jimenez, it's about something larger than herself. Jimenez laced up her sneakers Sunday to pay tribute to her late husband, Army Sgt. He was 27. Jimenez, a Huntington Beach, California resident who has run two other marathons, says she runs to keep the memory of her husband alive.
