One of the Internet's great promises is that it's the ultimate democratizer. It's open to everyone and allows all people to communicate. Facebook and Google have added new translation tools, but they take different approaches. But, so far, there have been several hitches in that plan. Some governments still censor the Internet. And why should an English speaker be barred from reading blogs written in Malagasy or Zulu? Meanwhile, Google plans to let computers do most of the work.
