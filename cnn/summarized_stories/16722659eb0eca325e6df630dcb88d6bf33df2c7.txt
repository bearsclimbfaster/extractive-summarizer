Japanese writer and director Hideo Nakata has been hailed as the modern master of macabre. The master of macabre, Hideo Nakata didn't start out in film aiming to make horror movies. His 1998 film "Ringu" -- adapted from a novel by Suzuki Koji, the Japanese Steven King -- redefined the horror genre, propelled Nakata into the international spotlight and soon had Hollywood calling. Born in Okayama, Japan, in 1961, Nakata enrolled at the University of Tokyo to study journalism.
